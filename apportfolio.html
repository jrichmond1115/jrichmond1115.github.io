AP Portfolio

Joseph Richmond

Welcome to my AP Portfolio!

This year for AP art, I started by looking for the idea of “resilience” abstractly in urban settings when I noticed the way colorful trash looked when juxtaposed against the urban landscape. This inspired me to use real trash and nature as a medium to make an abstract work situated around the way color stands out (1). This led me to explore more gritty understandings of beauty in urban settings, where color shines through conventional brokenness (2). On this journey, I began to create close relationships with the homeless who live on the streets of Cleveland, so I started exploring their resilience through portraiture, experimenting with mixed media and texture to show the emotion of people and their situations (3,4). As part of the dignity of these men that I wanted to reveal, I would show the guys or their friends a picture of the piece I made of them. I revised these pieces to capture the abstract essence of individuals like these who overcome hardship, experimenting with brushwork and saturation (5).

I then shifted this general idea to navigate the rest of my portfolio toward the resilience, hope, and perseverance that individuals and groups alike have during COVID-19. As we all have many mixed emotions during this time, I wanted to capture the overcoming we have of this hardship. I experimented with texture, color, the news, and people’s own personal experiences by starting abstractly(6-7) and moving to visuals of overcoming the situation individually yet as one body (7-10).

Slide 1: Trash to Trees

This piece represents the abstract resilience of the color in the city, juxtaposed with nature. Both parts bring their own qualities of hope and the installation of nature around the piece, followed by the removal of the piece, which creates an authentic dispersion of nature's resilence.

Slide 2: Urban Light

This piece is based on the essence that urban environments overcome other's prior judgements of location.

Slide 3: Charles

This piece is inspired by a homeless friend of mine, Charles, who lightens up the city with his stories and his smile. Symbolically, he radiates color.

Slide 4: Stoic

This piece is inspired by more homeless friends of mine, but particularly the essence of their resilence. The color and facial features are more important (and stand out more) than the dirt and grit of the background/cracks.

Slide 5: Abstract Resilience

This piece is based on the essence embodied through individuals who display the overcoming of their hardships like those I met on the streets.

Slide 6: In War

This piece represents the internal and external joy we having during the isolation we face during COVID-19.

Slide 7: From the Same Colors

There is a significant importance of community during COVID-19 and the way that we are stronger together, regardless of prior grudges, notions, etc. We leave a mark of our own color on someone else.

Slide 8: Digital Touch

We can still stay connected with each other despite the physical separation we face. The piece is meant to represent the joy we bring to each other amongst digital boundaries.

Slide 9: Good News

This piece is a reminder and focus toward positivity (in the news) during COVID-19. After selecting titles from weeks worth of newspaper, the "bad" news titles remain dirtied and hidden in the back, whereas the "good news" (titles like "keep them learning," "museums are more important than ever," and "alone together") represent the way we are still connected and have important impacts from our own homes.

Slide 10: Strong Individuals, Powerful Body

As a final AP project, I made this work a collaborative effort to get friends, family, etc, actually involved in art together. Via instagrams posts, I had individuals send me pictures of themselves however they'd like. All of these pictures were used in the piece and, from close distance, you can see everyone in there, but from afar, it is only an abstract face. We overcomes things together and work as one body.
